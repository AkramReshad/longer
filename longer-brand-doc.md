# LONGER — Electrolyte Dysfunction

## The Concept

An electrolyte drink brand that satirizes erectile dysfunction pharmaceutical branding. The product is a legitimate, high-quality electrolyte powder. The entire brand identity, packaging, content, and voice is a deadpan parody of ED medication — Cialis, Viagra, the whole category. The humor comes from total commitment to the bit. Never winking. Playing it completely straight.

**Tagline:** "Are you suffering from ED? Electrolyte Dysfunction."

## The Product

- Powdered electrolyte stick packs (not bottles — bottles are a scale play for later)
- Mineral profile modeled after Gatorlyte: ~490mg sodium, ~350mg potassium, ~120mg magnesium per serving
- No sugar, natural flavors, stevia or monk fruit sweetener
- The product needs to actually taste good — the joke gets trial, the formula gets retention

## Flavor Names

All flavors use the pharmaceutical "-afil" suffix (like sildenafil/tadalafil):

- **Blue Pill** — Blue Raspberry (hero SKU)
- **PED Pineapple** — "Performance Enhancing Drink"
- **Watermelonafil** — Watermelon
- **Grapeafil** — Grape
- **Mangoafil** — Mango
- **Hard Lemon** — Lemonade
- **Morning Wood Berry** — Mixed Berry, "for your morning workout"
- **The Finisher** — Fruit Punch
- **Sustained Release** — Watermelon, slow sip
- **Performance Anxiety Grape** — "for when the pressure is on"
- **Banana Long** — Banana

Not all need to launch. Start with 3-4.

## Brand Identity

- **Primary color:** Viagra blue (Pantone 2935 C)
- **Logo:** Blue feminine silhouette — head tilted back, back arched, suggestive but ambiguous. "LONGER" in pharmaceutical serif font beneath, "ELECTROLYTE DYSFUNCTION" in small caps below that.
- **Packaging aesthetic:** Clinical white, serif fonts, modeled after Cialis/Viagra packaging. Looks like medication, not an energy drink.
- **Tone:** Deadpan pharmaceutical. Never acknowledges the joke. Complete commitment.

## Packaging Details

- Real FDA-compliant Nutrition Facts panel on one side (required by law)
- Satirical "Active Ingredients" panel on the other side:
  - "Watermelonafil 490mg Sodium Complex"
  - "Potassium Citrate Extended Release 350mg"
  - "Magnesium Glycinate Sustained Formula 120mg"
- "Dosage: Take one (1) packet by mouth 30 minutes before physical activity."
- "Warning: If you are enduring longer than 4 hours, consult your training partner."

## Content Strategy

This is the actual business. The electrolytes are commodity. The moat is an AI-powered content engine that produces volume at near-zero marginal cost.

### AI Content Pipeline

- Pull trending viral videos and formats from TikTok
- Re-skin them through the LONGER brand voice using AI
- Output 2-4 videos per day
- Review weekly, boost top 2 performers with ~$100 each

### Launch Video Concept

- Open on a woman's silhouette in a doorway, red light, slow motion, breathy music
- Cut to a marathon runner hitting mile 20, legs giving out
- Cut back to her stepping forward
- Cut to him reaching for something — she hands him a stick pack of LONGER
- Pharmaceutical voiceover: "When the moment comes... How long will you last?"
- Fake medical disclaimer scrolls at the bottom at double speed

This can be shot with two people and an iPhone. Post before product exists to validate demand.

### Content Principles

- TikTok first — algorithm evaluates every video independently, no follower gate
- Volume over perfection in the first 30 days
- Hook in the first second — "Are you suffering from Electrolyte Dysfunction?"
- Trend jack aggressively — every trending format is a template for the brand
- Comment replies become new videos (engagement loops)
- Every fake medical disclaimer at the end of every video
- "Ask your trainer if Watermelonafil is right for you"

## Go-to-Market Order

1. **Content first.** Post 10-20 TikToks with no product. Test whether the bit resonates.
2. **Email capture.** Shopify landing page with logo, "Coming Soon," email signup. Link in bio.
3. **Co-packer quotes in parallel.** Send inquiry emails now to get pricing, MOQs, and timelines. Don't place an order yet.
4. **Validate demand.** If videos hit 100K+ views and comments say "where do I buy this" — green light.
5. **Place production order.** First run of stick packs.
6. **Launch DTC.** Shopify + TikTok Shop.

## Production & Distribution

### Co-Packer

- Contract manufacturer handles formulation, flavor development, production, and packaging
- You provide the mineral profile (Gatorlyte-like) and print-ready packaging artwork
- Co-packer's food scientist handles the actual formulation
- Target MOQ: under 10,000 units per SKU. 5,000 is good, 2,500 is great.
- Setup/formulation fee of $1,500-3,000 is normal
- Per-unit cost: ~$0.50-1.50 per stick pack at low volume
- Lead time: 4-8 weeks from formulation to first production run
- LA-area co-packers identified: Ibitta Enterprises (South Gate), Felbro Food Products (LA), Impackt Packaging Solutions (Inland Empire)

### Fulfillment

- Co-packer ships pallets to a 3PL (third-party logistics warehouse)
- 3PL handles individual order pick/pack/ship
- Options: ShipBob, ShipMonk, Deliverr — all have LA warehouses, integrate with Shopify
- TikTok's Fulfilled by TikTok program is worth exploring since TikTok is the primary channel
- Per-order cost: ~$3-5 for pick/pack plus shipping

### Pricing

- Sell at ~$2-3 per stick or $30-40 for a box of 16
- Margins are strong at these price points against $0.50-1.50 COGS

## Legal

- FDA labeling compliance: co-packer handles nutrition facts panel, regulatory consultant reviews for $1,500-3,000
- Satirical disclaimers are obviously satirical — not making medical claims
- Parodying a category, not a specific brand
- No proprietary formulation to infringe — electrolyte ratios aren't ownable
- Attorney reviews packaging in an afternoon

## Design Next Steps

- Have an Upwork/Fiverr designer clean up the AI-generated logo into a simplified vector
- Consistent Viagra blue throughout
- Simplify silhouette curves for small-scale stick pack printing
- Design stick pack artwork in the Cialis box aesthetic
- Get print-ready files to co-packer spec (vector, CMYK, bleed marks)
- Budget: $200-500 for packaging design

## Why This Works

- Electrolyte market is large and growing
- Product is commodity — zero defensibility at formulation level — so brand IS the moat
- The ED satire is inherently viral and shareable
- The pharmaceutical aesthetic differentiates on shelf
- The AI content pipeline means outproducing every competitor on volume at near-zero cost
- Startup cost is $5-10K to validate, not a bet-the-farm investment
- Comparable model: Liquid Death built a $1.4B valuation on water by making it entertaining
